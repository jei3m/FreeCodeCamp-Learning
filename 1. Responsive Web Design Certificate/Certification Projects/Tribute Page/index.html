Albert Einstein

The Life of a Genius

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized scientific thought with his theories of relativity. His work continues to influence modern physics and philosophy.

Key Achievements and Life Events:

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Germany to a family of non-observant Jews
  • 1895 - Attempted early admission to ETH Zürich but failed the entrance exam
  • 1896 - Successfully enrolled at ETH Zürich after completing his secondary education
  • 1905 - Published four groundbreaking papers in his "miracle year":
    • Photoelectric effect (won Nobel Prize for this)
    • Brownian motion
    • Special relativity
    • Mass-energy equivalence (E = mc²)
  • 1915 - Completed the General Theory of Relativity
  • 1921 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1933 - Emigrated to the United States due to Nazi persecution
  • 1939 - Wrote the famous letter to President Roosevelt about atomic weapons
  • 1955 - Passed away in Princeton, leaving an unfinished unified field theory

Famous Quotes:

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."

"Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance, you must keep moving."
